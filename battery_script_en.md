# Conscientiousness Assessment Battery — Administration Script

> **Duration:** ~45 minutes
> **Format:** Zoom call — Assessor reads aloud, candidate responds verbally
> **Notation:** **Bold** = Read aloud to candidate | *Italic* = Internal instructions for assessor

---

## OPENING (2 minutes)

*Start the Zoom recording. Confirm the candidate's name and the position they are being assessed for. Note the start time on the answer sheet.*

**"Hello [Candidate Name], thank you for joining today. My name is [Your Name] and I'm from [Agency Name]. Today we'll go through a structured assessment that helps us understand your work style and preferences. There are no right or wrong answers — we're interested in how you naturally approach work."**

**"The session will take about 45 minutes and has three parts: first, a set of statements where you'll rank which ones describe you best; second, some work scenarios where you'll tell me what you'd do; and third, a few interview questions about your past experiences."**

**"Do you have any questions before we begin?"**

*Answer any questions. If the candidate asks what the assessment measures, say: "It looks at various aspects of your work style and personality." Do not mention conscientiousness specifically.*

`[CHECKPOINT: MINUTE 2 — BEGIN SECTION A]`

## SECTION A: FORCED-CHOICE PERSONALITY INVENTORY (10 minutes)

*Read the instructions to the candidate:*

**"In this first section, I'm going to read you groups of three statements. For each group, I'd like you to tell me which statement is MOST like you — the one that describes you best — and which is LEAST like you. The remaining statement will automatically be your middle choice."**

**"There are no right or wrong answers. Just go with your genuine first reaction. Ready?"**

*For each block: Read all three statements clearly. Wait for the candidate to choose MOST and LEAST. Record their ranking on the answer sheet (1st/2nd/3rd for each statement).*

## SECTION B: SITUATIONAL JUDGMENT TEST (12 minutes)

*Read the instructions:*

**"Now I'm going to describe some work situations. For each one, I'll give you four possible actions. Please tell me which action you would be MOST LIKELY to actually take — not what you think is the 'right' answer, but what you would genuinely do if this happened to you."**

**"Ready?"**

---

### Scenario 1

**"You have just taken over a role from a colleague who left suddenly. Their project files are scattered across multiple folders — some on shared drives, some on their desktop. There are no notes explaining the file naming system. Your manager needs a status update by end of week."**

**"What would you most likely do?"**

**"A: Start working on the deliverables immediately using whatever files you can find, and sort through the mess later."**

**"B: Spend the first day creating a complete inventory of all files, renaming and organizing them into a logical structure before doing any deliverable work."**

**"C: Ask a colleague who worked with the previous person to walk you through the files and point out what's important."**

**"D: Email your manager explaining that the files are disorganized and ask for a deadline extension."**

*Record choice: ___*

---

### Scenario 2

**"You are reviewing a monthly report that your team submits to a key client. You notice that a data point from last month's report was slightly incorrect — the error went unnoticed and the client made no complaint. Correcting it now would mean admitting the previous mistake."**

**"A: Leave it alone — the client didn't notice, and bringing it up now could damage trust."**

**"B: Quietly fix the number in this month's report without mentioning the previous error."**

**"C: Inform your manager about the error and suggest sending a correction notice to the client, along with updated data."**

**"D: Mention it casually to a colleague and let them decide whether to escalate."**

*Record choice: ___*

---

### Scenario 3

**"You have completed a project ahead of schedule. Your deliverable meets all the stated requirements and your manager has approved it. **"A: Submit the approved version. It meets requirements, and you have other tasks waiting."**

**"B: Implement both improvements without telling anyone, then submit the enhanced version."**

**"C: Propose the improvements to your manager, explain the value they'd add, and offer to implement them within 3 days — asking them to decide."**

**"D: Make a note of the improvements for the next project iteration and move on."**

## SECTION C: STRUCTURED BEHAVIORAL INTERVIEW — STAR FORMAT (18 minutes)

*Instructions for assessor: Allow the candidate ~3 minutes per question. Use the follow-up probes if the candidate's initial answer is too vague or doesn't cover all STAR elements. Mark the STAR completeness checkboxes and BARS score on the answer sheet. Write down key phrases verbatim when possible.*

---

### Question 1 — Competence (C1)
*~3 minutes*

**"Tell me about a time you were assigned a task or project that was outside your area of expertise. How did you approach it, and what was the outcome?"**

*Listen for: Proactive learning, structured approach to skill gaps, confidence in execution.*

*If the answer is vague, probe:*
- **"What specific steps did you take to get up to speed?"**
- **"How did you know when you were ready to execute?"**

*Record: BARS score (1-5): ___ | STAR: S☐ T☐ A☐ R☐ | Key phrases: ___________*

---

### Question 2 — Order (C2)
*~3 minutes*

**"Describe how you organize your work when you're juggling multiple projects or tasks with different deadlines. Walk me through your actual system."**

*Listen for: Named tools/systems, prioritization logic, review cadence, handling disruptions.*

*If the answer is vague, probe:*
- **"What happens when a new urgent task gets added to your plate?"**
- **"Can you describe your actual tool or system in detail?"**

*Record: BARS score (1-5): ___ | STAR: S☐ T☐ A☐ R☐ | Key phrases: ___________*

---

### Question 3 — Dutifulness (C3)
*~3 minutes*

**"Tell me about a time you had to deliver bad news or admit a mistake to a manager or client. What exactly did you do, and when did you do it?"**

*Listen for: Speed of disclosure, ownership vs. blame, proposed solution, prevention measures.*

*If the answer is vague, probe:*
- **"How long after discovering the problem did you report it?"**
- **"What would have happened if you hadn't said anything?"**

*Record: BARS score (1-5): ___ | STAR: S☐ T☐ A☐ R☐ | Key phrases: ___________*

---

### Question 4 — Achievement Striving (C4)
*~3 minutes*

**"Tell me about a goal you set for yourself that went beyond what was required or expected of you. Why did you pursue it, and what happened?"**

*Listen for: Self-initiated (not manager-assigned), measurable target, tracking progress, intrinsic motivation.*

*If the answer is vague, probe:*
- **"Did anyone ask you to do this, or was it entirely self-initiated?"**
- **"How did you measure your progress?"**

*Record: BARS score (1-5): ___ | STAR: S☐ T☐ A☐ R☐ | Key phrases: ___________*

---

### Question 5 — Self-Discipline (C5)
*~3 minutes*

**"Tell me about a time you had to work on something that was extremely boring, repetitive, or tedious — something you really didn't want to do. How did you get through it?"**

*Listen for: Specific focus strategies, quality maintenance, self-awareness of attention patterns, no corner-cutting.*

*If the answer is vague, probe:*
- **"What specifically helped you maintain focus?"**
- **"Did you consider cutting corners? What stopped you?"**

*Record: BARS score (1-5): ___ | STAR: S☐ T☐ A☐ R☐ | Key phrases: ___________*

---

### Question 6 — Deliberation (C6)
*~3 minutes*

**"Describe a high-stakes decision you had to make at work. Walk me through your decision-making process — how did you evaluate your options before choosing?"**

*Listen for: Multiple alternatives considered, structured evaluation, stakeholder input, risk assessment, bias awareness.*

*If the answer is vague, probe:*
- **"What alternatives did you consider and reject?"**
- **"Did you consult anyone? How did you weigh conflicting advice?"**
